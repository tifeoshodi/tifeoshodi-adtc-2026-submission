CASSAVA DISEASES AND MANAGEMENT
Cassava Mosaic Disease (CMD) is a viral disease spread by whiteflies or through infected cuttings. Symptoms include a mosaic pattern on leaves, stunting, and reduced yield. To manage CMD, use disease-free, certified cuttings and plant resistant varieties like TMS 30572. Uproot and burn infected plants early in the season.

Cassava Bacterial Blight (CBB) causes water-soaked angular spots on leaves, which later turn brown and dry. It can cause severe defoliation. Control CBB by crop rotation, using healthy planting materials, and avoiding movement through wet fields to prevent bacterial spread.

MAIZE CULTIVATION BEST PRACTICES
Maize requires well-drained soils and a good supply of nutrients, particularly nitrogen. Planting should align with the onset of the rainy season. Plant seeds at a depth of 2-5 cm, with a spacing of 75 cm between rows and 25 cm between plants. 
Fall Armyworm is a major pest for maize. Scout fields regularly. Early detection is key. Use approved biological or chemical pesticides if damage exceeds the economic threshold (e.g., 20% of plants infested).

YAM STORAGE AND HANDLING
Yams are highly perishable if not stored correctly. After harvest, yams should be cured in a shaded, well-ventilated area for a few days to heal surface wounds. Store tubers in a cool, dry place, such as a traditional yam barn where they are tied to vertical poles, ensuring adequate airflow.
Inspect stored yams regularly and remove any rotting tubers immediately to prevent the spread of fungal infections.
